Copperis bett and latten are proper to
cast & at the right heat when they cast, while stirring them in the
crucible, a very white flame & that the bath is very
liquid.

Some cast in well ground ceruse, pour
le; others in flour moistened several times with
oil& g & dried in the
sun.

Others, for casting in lead, old letters of
printers or types for the
setting of letters.

Others put into the tin or lead a substance of
fixed quicksilver which makes it run.
